Welcome

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, specializing in computational climate science, hydrosystems, and ecohydrology. My research focuses on understanding the complex interactions between climate, water, and ecosystems across multiple spatiotemporal scales.

About Me

I hold a Ph.D. in Civil Engineering (Hydrosystems) from the University of Houston. My dissertation explored catchment ecohydrological processes and their interactions across various scales. I also have a Master of Advanced Studies in Sustainable Water Resources from ETH Zurich and an M.Sc. in Water Resources Engineering and Management from Addis Ababa University.

My research interests encompass catchment ecohydrology, urban ecohydrology, medium to large scale hydrological/water resources modeling, water resource systems, hydrological extremes, dynamic risk management, and the resilience of water infrastructures.
