Module Components

SurfaceRunoff

  • Calculates surface runoff based on soil properties, moisture levels, and urban vs. rural settings.
  • Takes into account frozen soil layers and the impact of snow and ice on runoff.

Infiltration

  • Computes infiltration into the surface soil layer minus the evaporation.
  • Handles partitioning of moisture fluxes between soil and surface water (h2osfc).
  • Considers modified infiltration schemes and water inputs from various sources, including irrigation.

WaterTable

  • Calculates the water table, accounting for aquifer recharge without drainage.
  • Determines the layer containing the water table and computes the specific yield for the aquifer.
  • Handles perched water tables and frost tables, updating soil moisture and ice content based on condensation and sublimation processes.

Drainage

  • Calculates subsurface drainage, including both bottom and topographic control drainage.
  • Considers factors like hydraulic conductivity, soil matric potential, and the effect of ice on soil permeability.
  • Uses different approaches for vegetated vs. urban land units and adjusts water table depth accordingly.

DrainageVSFM

  • Specific version of the drainage calculation that uses a different soil moisture movement model.
  • Includes additional variables and parameters for more detailed simulation of soil hydrology processes.

Key Variables and Parameters

  • Bounds: Represents the spatial bounds of the calculation.
  • SoilHydrologyType, SoilStateType, WaterfluxType: Custom types that encapsulate various hydrological, state, and water flux parameters.
  • dtime: Time step for the calculations.
  • num_hydrologyc, filter_hydrologyc, num_urbanc, filter_urbanc: Arrays and counters for filtering and processing hydrological and urban column points.
  • h2osoi_liq, h2osoi_ice, h2osfc: Soil moisture variables representing liquid water, ice content, and surface water.
  • zwt, zwt_perched: Variables for the depth of the water table and perched water table.

Detailed Calculations

  • Surface Runoff: Considers soil moisture, urban impervious surfaces, and snow cover to compute runoff.
  • Infiltration: Uses soil properties, topographic features, and water inputs to calculate how much water infiltrates the soil.
  • Water Table Calculation: Determines the depth of the water table based on aquifer recharge, specific yield, and soil moisture conditions.
  • Subsurface Drainage: Accounts for various soil layers, ice content, and hydraulic conductivity to compute drainage.
